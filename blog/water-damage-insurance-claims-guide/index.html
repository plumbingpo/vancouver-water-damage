Filing Insurance Claims for Water Damage in Vancouver, WA

Home / Blog / Filing Insurance Claims for Water Damage in Vancouver, WA

Filing an insurance claim for water damage can be confusing, especially when you're already dealing with the disruption of a flooded or damaged property. Understanding how the insurance claims process works, what's covered, and how to maximize your claim can make a significant difference in your financial recovery. This guide from Vancouver Restoration Co helps Vancouver, WA homeowners navigate the insurance process with confidence.

What's Typically Covered

Most standard homeowner's insurance policies cover sudden and accidental water damage. This includes burst pipes, appliance failures (washing machines, water heaters, dishwashers), ice dam damage, accidental overflow from sinks or bathtubs, and sudden HVAC system failures. Coverage typically extends to the cost of water extraction, structural drying, material replacement, and related restoration expenses including temporary living expenses if your home is uninhabitable.

What's Typically NOT Covered

Standard policies usually exclude gradual water damage from slow leaks or deferred maintenance, flooding from external water sources (requiring separate flood insurance), sewage backup (requiring a specific endorsement), foundation seepage due to groundwater, and damage from neglected maintenance issues. Understanding these exclusions before a loss occurs allows you to add endorsements or obtain separate policies to fill coverage gaps.

Steps to File a Successful Claim

When water damage occurs, take these steps to protect your claim: Document everything with photos and video before cleanup begins. Contact your insurance company promptly to report the loss. Take reasonable steps to prevent additional damage (mitigation). Keep damaged items until the adjuster has inspected them. Get professional restoration from an IICRC-certified company. Keep all receipts related to the loss including temporary repairs and living expenses. Be present during the adjuster's inspection and point out all damage.
